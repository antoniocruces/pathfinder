Correlations

In PATHFINDER, it is understood as correlation the frequency of two different types of primary record metainfo match in a record set. It allows, for example, to find out how many times a particular actor curates exhibitions showing a concrete work of art. It use transinformation as measurement method.

True to its name, PATHFINDER "traces" the path of relationships linking non-similar primary record types using a directed graph. Using it and related found metainfo a "path" is created, built as a collection of sucessive steps making the link. You just nedd to specify starting and end points of the route. Elements to be considered are shown below.

Source Route starting point, as in actor → artwork author → artwork. It show the types of primary registry linked to the metainformation acting as relationship between them.
Target Route end point, as in exhibition → curator → actor. It show the types of primary registry linked to the metainformation acting as relationship between them.
Recalculate Layout of the route. If one or more steps have been removed, rebuild the original route.
Route graph Diagram of the route. It shows an information window depicting the drawing of the route in a directed graph.
Route steps When you press one of them, it is deleted and will not be used in the final calculation of correlations. The colors of the elements are as follows:
P actor, B catalog, C company, E entity, X exhibition, A artwork, ? relation between records. Relationship fields are in gray and can not be deleted.
