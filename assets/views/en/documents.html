Know the main PATHFINDER operation ways, its scientific and technical foundations, and learn to use the resources offered by the tool.

What is

Welcome to the world of quantitative analysis of qualitative information. Welcome to the world of the new knowledge, the big data and the geographical approach generation. Welcome, in brief, to a tool to open new paths in the research about temporary expositions of artistic content world and catalogues by digital approach.

PATHFINDER has been developed as an auxiliary tool. It does not work miracles. It does not solve problems. It simply provides the user with a complete set of keys to open closed spaces which hide complex structures of tangled network, of not so visible relations, of unsuspected routes, of intuitive but not proven relationships between the institutions and companies involved in the expositional process, people who make the exhibited works, those who intervene in the commission, the catalographic publications and the artistic work itself.

In order to maximize efficiency, the application is designed so the most of the computational effort is done by the user’s computer and not a remote machine, so it is convenient to use well-equipped devices and updated software. But in reality, there is nothing special about their functional demands, and practically any relatively new equipment with a well-maintained operating system (we live in the 21st century, ladies and gentlemen… do not continue to use operating systems and browsers from the late 20th century; for a third millennium researcher this should never be a subterfuge) you will be able to carry out the operations without any problem. Check in the requirements section if the computer you are using is correctly prepared and will offer you a correct user experience.

Specialized tool

We will try to build a brief account of the gestation and implementation of an exciting idea: to put within reach of any researcher in the field of Humanities (especially that of Digital Art History) a simple but efficient tool to discover, in the great tide of relevant data when studying temporary exhibitions of artistic content and their catalogues, Ariadne’s thread that leads to logical, reasonable and scientific explanations.

When the possibility arose in 2004 that the research group iArtHis_LAB, linked to the Department of History of Art of the University of Malaga, could participate in a competition of ideas organized by the BBVA Foundation to finance innovative projects in various scientific fields, it did not cease to seem like a remote possibility to gain access to one of the winning positions. iArtHis_LAB is a laboratory of research, innovation and training for the development of digital studies on artistic culture, and in particular for the progress and consolidation of the History of Digital Art in Spain. Working in the still new world of the History of Digital Art and Digital Humanities requires mixed profiles, hybrid training and, above all, a large dose of life force, a desire to progress, and a great capacity for work. Moreover, all this must be articulated in a balances whole, where the development tactic does not hinder the work of the strategy.

The research group combines youth and experience, vitality and passion for reasoned and reasonable advancement. Their goal is to go far and contribute, as far as possible, to making Art History a leading subject at the beginning of the Third Millennium. There is no shortage of winnings, and there is no shortage of human resources. But since almost everything remains to be done in this area, those who want to participate can contact the group. All initiatives are welcomed.

Deep down, the idea behind PATHFINDER is extremely simple. Every time someone from the world of Humanities (and, in particular, from the world of Art History) tries to tackle the problem of supporting their research on solid foundations, they encounter the same pitfalls that have existed there since the times of Scylla and Charybdis. Nothing could be further from a good concept of “science” that “this statement comes from this one or that one, and, therefore, it must be true, because they have studied the subject a lot. Let no “good thinker” be deceived: it is an attitude —unfortunately— much more usual than one might believe.

The PATHFINDER development team is truly and very concerned about this situation. For this reason, it was decided to design an application that could offer both the less experienced researcher in the handling of computer artifacts and the more experienced one in those areas, resources to work without the need for external dependencies. That is to say, a mechanism that could be used, after minimal training, just like today any art historian uses a word processing program to compose their articles or an image retouching program to prepare their illustrations. It should be so easy to understand that it does not require specific training except, of course, the minimum necessary to be able to use its full potential. At the same time, it would have to be versatile, efficient and undemanding in terms of hardware and software requirements.

And both the project created for the event organized by FBBVA, Exhibitium, and its subsequent development, ArtCatalogs (HAR2014-51915-P), offered an unprecedented opportunity to put the idea into practice. Both were based on the data framework created by specific applications that collected the information captures by human or electronic means and unified it into a coherent structure. Both, too, and prior to the indispensable security safeguards, were prepared to remotely offer the result of their information collections. It was, therefore, and so to speak, to “draw” the appropriate container to collect the water that overflowed both tanks.

Purpose

PATHFINDER is an online application with client-server architecture that forms part of the Exhibitium and ArtCatalogs (HAR2014-51915-P) projects, developed by the research group iArtHis_LAB, from the Department of Art History of the University of Malaga, Spain. It has been conceived as a complement to obtain additional statistic and geographic information and generate new knowledge from the data stored in ExpoFinder, which is part of Exhibitium.

The aim of PATHFINDER is to show the possibilities provided by the geographical treatment of data on temporary exhibitions of artistic content and catalogues compiled both types of registration in ExpoFinder, using different techniques to represent the densities of organizing, financing or promoting entities, exhibition centers, actors (artists, curators, illustrators, editors, …) and the relationships between all these elements.

The application is designed to be able to locate locations and make comparative studies between them. It is possible to filter the data by years of beginning of exhibitions, by number of events organized, by locations and by other characteristics.

Why an online app?

Web or online applications are programs that provide some tools to every user who is connected to the internet and who access them. All this work is done on the web, only using an updated browser as a resource. This new conception of the use of the network as a tool not only for the dissemination of information, but for the creation of the same, goes far beyond the titanic struggles carried out in the last decade between the software development corporations that demand a fair retribution for their work, and the users that accessed these services without having acquired an original license committing a crime punishable by law.

PATHFINDER has been conceived as a non-finalist tool. That is to say, its purpose is not to carry out the user’s work, but to provide it with mechanism that allow it to obtain its own conclusions through experimentation. The vast majority of computing power resides in the costumer’s equipment, so it is absolutely essential to use a computer we equipped with volatile memory (RAM) and a very updated browser.

Precisely because it is an application with a clear utilitarian objective, the aesthetic appearance of the PATHFINDER user interface is minimalist, not at all prone to abuse of bells and whistles that may distract from their functionality.

What kind of application is PATHFINDER?

PATHFINDER is a hybrid web application or mashup. It is a tool that invokes or “calls” the results obtained from others, in order to reuse its content and / or functionality. That is to say, it is a system that simplifies the access to the results of others visualized in a single graphical interface all of them, conferring them unity and homogeneity. The term itself implies easy and fast integration, often using several open APIs and data sources to produce rich results, which may not necessarily have been the original reason for producing the primary source of data. Ontology

Below is provided basic information on the various components of the information system called ExpoFinder in order to facilitate the process of exporting data from it. In this document, the term information system is used in the same sense as described by Encyclopaedia Britannica: "an integrated set of components for collecting, storing, and processing data and for delivering information, knowledge, and digital products".

Primary records.
Main record types storing basic typologies of the system, following the statement of the ExpoFinder operating document:
  • Entities [entity ENT]. An entity (according to DRAE, in Spanish) is understood as "any corporation, company, institution, etc., taken as a legal person". In this case, any organization with a legal entity that is used in the context of ExpoFinder, either as a source of RSS/Atom or HTML data, from the results of which the system explores the Web in search of information on temporary exhibitions, will be considered as such, as well as any other organization that acts as a support organizer or financer of an exhibition, of its catalog or to which a person is linked in a work, research or academic way.
  • Actors [actor ACT]. An actor in the context of ExpoFinder is understood to be one who interacts with catalogues or exhibitions as author, editor, illustrator, curator, loan or other circumstances, and who may or may not be related to entities.
  • Catalogues [book CAT]. A catalog in the context of ExpoFinder is understood as any article, book, web page or text in general that is used to illustrate the visitor about a specific exhibition. Its “natural” use is to store information about its “catalogues”.
  • Companies [company COM]. A company in the context of ExpoFinder is understood as any organization whose relationship with entities, exhibitions, publications or individuals can be defined EXCLUSIVELY in commercial or business terms. This distinction, therefore, is not based on the public, private or mixed character of the same, nor on the fact that its purposes include or do not include a profit motive, but on the fact that the RELATIONSHIP is by a profitable motive.
  • Exhibitions [exhibition EXP]. An exhibition in the context of ExpoFinder is understood as any event in which a set of works of an artistic nature is exhibited before the public or whose intention is to disseminate knowledge of the academic discipline of Art History.
  • Artworks [artwork OBR]. An artwork en el contexto de ExpoFinder is understood as any manifestation of human activity through which the real is interpreted or the imagined is captured with plastic, linguistic or sound resources.
Metainformations.
It contains any additional data that allows to characterize in an unequivocal way any record. For example, its physical location, a date describing its beginning or end, or an e-mail address. In reality, and as a whole, they act as singular descriptors of any element of the record table. At the same time, metainformations make it possible to establish the links between the different types of registers, so that it is possible, for example, to locate the people who curate an exhibition or participate in it as artists.
Taxonomies.
They contains logical categories being assigned to a certain record. Such categories are shared with others, so that they can be used to classify in significant sets the exhibitions, companies, persons, entities and publications.

Taxonomies

According to the vocabulary of Wordpress, or WP, (used in the development of ExpoFinder as an application framework), taxonomy is understood as a mechanism of classification and grouping of different primary information-bearing records (posts in the context) based on specific words or semantic units. If developer needs so, they can be a hierarchical structure. It is possible, using this application, to download the information, including terms, of all taxonomies used in ExpoFinder. Its organization follows the scheme below. Be conscious of two facts: the symbol ® is used to denote the pertenence of terms to a controlled vocabulary, and the symbol § means that the taxonomy has a hierarchical structure:

  • Entities:
    • Typology ®. Basic characterization (acting as…) of the entity in the information system.
    • Ownership ®. Simple grouping of the entity based on whether the owner adheres to public law, private one or a mixture of both.
    • Keywords. Identifiable and / or descriptive terms usable by the user editor of the record at will.
  • Actors:
    • Activity ®. Known tasks played by an actor having a significant role in the context of application data.
  • Catalogs:
    • Editorial ®. Company or organism acting as editor of the publication.
    • Catalog typology ®. Generic classification of the catalog according its contents or its relationship to catalogued exhibition.
  • Companies:
    • ISIC4 Category ®. Classification of the company based on its main activity. Follows the rules issued by United Nations Statistics Division (UNSTAT). International Standard Industrial Classification of All Economic Activities, Rev.4.
  • Exhibitions:
    • Exhibition type ®. Basic characterization of the exhibition according to a hyerarchy based in Michael Belcher and Ángela García Blanco works.
    • Artworks type ®§. Typologies of the shown artworks according to categories of the Getty Foundation thesauri.
    • Theme ®§. Topics of the shown artworks according to categories of the Getty Foundation thesauri.
    • Movement ®. Art movements or trendings susceptible to be considered as subscribed by the exhibited artworks according to categories of the Getty Foundation thesauri.
    • Period ®. Temporal categorization of the exhibited artworks based on large chronological periods (centuries and historical ages).
  • Artworks:
    • Artworks type ®§. Typologies of the artwork according to categories of the Getty Foundation thesauri.
    • Theme ®§. Topics of the artwork according to categories of the Getty Foundation thesauri.
    • Movement ®. Art movements or trendings susceptible to be considered as subscribed by the artwork according to categories of the Getty Foundation thesauri.
    • Period ®. Temporal categorization of the artwork based on large chronological periods (centuries and historical ages).

NOTICE: Artwork taxonomies are shared with exhibitions. Any term registered in one of them will be visible and usable in both types of record.

Metadata

Each primary record type (entities, actors, catalogs, companies, exhibitions and works) has a notorius amount of associated meta-information, defining its specific features. Since it is not treated purely of a grouping or classification mechanism, such meta information is not considered as a taxonomy. The formal inventory of the additional fields assigned to each type of record is summarized below.

Some conventional signs (symbology) are used in the lists shown below:

  1. Symbol ↺ meaning that the field is self-referenced, that is, it searches among the primary records whose abbreviation is shown after the symbol. If it is so, the field follows the rule ID + :, that is, each value for that field is prefixed by a number matching the value ID of the primary record aforementioned, followed by a colon (:) and a blank space.
  2. Symbol ⋇ meaning that the field value is verified by Geonames. In case the field is intended to store the name of a town follows the rule ; * 2, meaning tthat each value must to contain 3 items separated by semicolon (;), according the following scheme: municipality ; region or state ; country. n case the field is intended to store the name of a country it simply contains this value with no separators.
  3. Symbol ⨹ meaning that the field value is verified by Google Maps.
  4. Symbol ⊠ meaning that the field value is read-only and is automatically calculated.
  5. Symbol ⊗ meaning that the field value has been automatically got or calculated by means of Beagle process.

Fields containing a date use the following conventions:

  1. UNIQUE DATES (only one field)
    1. If the whole date is known it uses the scheme [four digits year]-[two digits month]-[two digits day].
    2. If only month and year is known it uses the scheme [four digits year]-[two digits month]-01.
    3. If only year is known it uses the scheme [four digits year]-01-01.
    4. Is date is before the beginning of our era (b.C.) year will be prefixed by a negative sigh [-].
    5. If date refers to a century, it uses 25 year fractions, and will be valid the numeral containing fraction start following the schema below:
      • NN00 = first quarter of NN+1 century
      • NN25 = second quarter of NN+1 century
      • NN50 = third quarter of NN+1 century
      • NN75 = fourth quarter of NN+1 century
      Thus, for example, 1725 will be used to express the second quarter of the eighteenth century. Accuracy below 0.25 of a century will not be admitted.
  2. PAIRED DATES (start and end)
    1. If the two complete dates are known both data follows the scheme [four digits year]-[two digits month]-[two digits day].
    2. If only month and year of one of the two dates is known both data follows the scheme
      1. If start date is known, end date will be [four digits year]-[two digits month]-[last possible day of month].
      2. If end date is known, start date will be [four digits year]-[two digits month]-01.
    3. If only year of one of both dates is known both data follows the scheme
      1. If start date is known, end date will be [four digits year]-12-31.
      2. If end date is known, start date will be [four digits year]-01-01.

NOTICE:

  1. CRC32B Identifier automatically calculates a hash using CRC32B algorithm to analyze the main significant record fields. It is used to prevent the system from trying to save as record the same found exposure twice.
  2. ExpoFinder uses a list of keywords to be found for Beagle inside a located text. They are used as a measure unit of the reliability of a finding by means of a calculations combination, including absolute and relative weight of the found keywords (frequency of appearance inside analyzed text), according to a defined value ranking for each of them.
  3.   4. Posible significant marks (PMS) identifies and extracts a part of the context of the keywords mentioned in note number 2 in order to help data recorders in their selection and filtering task.

  6. Visits range field has the following options: < 100, 100 a 499 (100 to 499), 500 a 1.000 (500 to 1,000), > 1.000 (> 1,000).

Access type field has the following options: No disponible (Not available), Gratis para todos (Free for everyone), Gratis según criterios de edad (Free according to age criteria), Gratis según criterios numéricos (Free according to numeric criteria), Gratis según criterios de nacionalidad o ciudadanía (Free according to nationality or citizenship criteria), De pago en todos los casos (Payment in all cases), Sistema mixto (Mixed system), Tarifa reducida (Reduced fare).
  8. Relationship type field has the following options: Main venue, Secondary venue, Auxiliary venue.
