What is PATHFINDER

Welcome to the world of quantitative analysis of qualitative information. Welcome to the world of the new knowledge, the big data and the geographical approach generation. Welcome, in brief, to a tool to open new paths in the research about temporary expositions of artistic content world and catalogues by digital approach.

PATHFINDER has been developed as an auxiliary tool. It does not work miracles. It does not solve problems. It simply provides the user with a complete set of keys to open closed spaces which hide complex structures of tangled network, of not so visible relations, of unsuspected routes, of intuitive but not proven relationships between the institutions and companies involved in the expositional process, people who make the exhibited works, those who intervene in the commission, the catalographic publications and the artistic work itself.

In order to maximize efficiency, the application is designed so the most of the computational effort is done by the user’s computer and not a remote machine, so it is convenient to use well-equipped devices and updated software. But in reality, there is nothing special about their functional demands, and practically any relatively new equipment with a well-maintained operating system (we live in the 21st century, ladies and gentlemen… do not continue to use operating systems and browsers from the late 20th century; for a third millennium researcher this should never be a subterfuge) you will be able to carry out the operations without any problem. Check in the requirements section if the computer you are using is correctly prepared and will offer you a correct user experience.

Specialized tool

We will try to build a brief account of the gestation and implementation of an exciting idea: to put within reach of any researcher in the field of Humanities (especially that of Digital Art History) a simple but efficient tool to discover, in the great tide of relevant data when studying temporary exhibitions of artistic content and their catalogues, Ariadne’s thread that leads to logical, reasonable and scientific explanations.

When the possibility arose in 2004 that the research group iArtHis_LAB, linked to the Department of History of Art of the University of Malaga, could participate in a competition of ideas organized by the BBVA Foundation to finance innovative projects in various scientific fields, it did not cease to seem like a remote possibility to gain access to one of the winning positions. iArtHis_LAB is a laboratory of research, innovation and training for the development of digital studies on artistic culture, and in particular for the progress and consolidation of the History of Digital Art in Spain. Working in the still new world of the History of Digital Art and Digital Humanities requires mixed profiles, hybrid training and, above all, a large dose of life force, a desire to progress, and a great capacity for work. Moreover, all this must be articulated in a balances whole, where the development tactic does not hinder the work of the strategy.

The research group combines youth and experience, vitality and passion for reasoned and reasonable advancement. Their goal is to go far and contribute, as far as possible, to making Art History a leading subject at the beginning of the Third Millennium. There is no shortage of winnings, and there is no shortage of human resources. But since almost everything remains to be done in this area, those who want to participate can contact the group. All initiatives are welcomed.

At bottom, the idea behind PATHFINDER is extremely simple. Every time someone from the world of Humanities (and, in particular, from the world of Art History) tries to tackle the problem of supporting their research on solid foundations, they encounter the same pitfalls that have existed there since the times of Scylla and Charybdis. Nothing could be further from a good concept of “science” that “this statement comes from this one or that one, and, therefore, it must be true, because they have studied the subject a lot. Let no “good thinker” be deceived: it is an attitude —unfortunately— much more usual than one might believe.

The PATHFINDER development team is truly and very concerned about this situation. For this reason, it was decided to design an application that could offer both the less experienced researcher in the handling of computer artifacts and the more experienced one in those areas, resources to work without the need for external dependencies. That is to say, a mechanism that could be used, after minimal training, just like today any art historian uses a word processing program to compose their articles or an image retouching program to prepare their illustrations. It should be so easy to understand that it does not require specific training except, of course, the minimum necessary to be able to use its full potential. At the same time, it would have to be versatile, efficient and undemanding in terms of hardware and software requirements.

And both the project created for the event organized by FBBVA, Exhibitium, and its subsequent development, ArtCatalogs (HAR2014-51915-P), offered an unprecedented opportunity to put the idea into practice. Both were based on the data framework created by specific applications that collected the information captures by human or electronic means and unified it into a coherent structure. Both, too, and prior to the indispensable security safeguards, were prepared to remotely offer the result of their information collections. It was, therefore, and so to speak, to “draw” the appropriate container to collect the water that overflowed both tanks.

Purpose

PATHFINDER is an online application with client-server architecture that forms part of the Exhibitium and ArtCatalogs (HAR2014-51915-P) projects, developed by the research group iArtHis_LAB, from the Department of Art History of the University of Malaga, Spain. It has been conceived as a complement to obtain additional statistic and geographic information and generate new knowledge from the data stored in ExpoFinder, which is part of Exhibitium.

The aim of PATHFINDER is to show the possibilities provided by the geographical treatment of data on temporary exhibitions of artistic content and catalogues compiled both types of registration in ExpoFinder, using different techniques to represent the densities of organizing, financing or promoting entities, exhibition centers, actors (artists, curators, illustrators, editors, …) and the relationships between all these elements.

The application is designed to be able to locate locations and make comparative studies between them. It is possible to filter the data by years of beginning of exhibitions, by number of events organized, by locations and by other characteristics.

Why an online app?

Web or online applications are programs that provide some tools to every user who is connected to the internet and who access them. All this work is done on the web, only using an updated browser as a resource. This new conception of the use of the network as a tool not only for the dissemination of information, but for the creation of the same, goes far beyond the titanic struggles carried out in the last decade between the software development corporations that demand a fair retribution for their work, and the users that accessed these services without having acquired an original license committing a crime punishable by law.

PATHFINDER has been conceived as a non-finalist tool. That is to say, its purpose is not to carry out the user’s work, but to provide it with mechanism that allow it to obtain its own conclusions through experimentation. The vast majority of computing power resides in the costumer’s equipment, so it is absolutely essential to use a computer we equipped with volatile memory (RAM) and a very updated browser.

Precisely because it is an application with a clear utilitarian objective, the aesthetic appearance of the PATHFINDER user interface is minimalist, not at all prone to abuse of bells and whistles that may distract from their functionality.

What kind of application is PATHFINDER?

PATHFINDER is a hybrid web application or mashup. It is a tool that invokes or “calls” the results obtained from others, in order to reuse its content and / or functionality. That is to say, it is a system that simplifies the access to the results of others visualized in a single graphical interface all of them, conferring them unity and homogeneity. The term itself implies easy and fast integration, often using several open APIs and data sources to produce rich results, which may not necessarily have been the original reason for producing the primary source of data. Ontology

The following provides basic information on the various components of the information system called ExpoFinder in order to facilitate the process of exporting data from it. In this document, the term information system is used in the same sense as described by Encyclopaedia Britannica:

"an integrated set of components for collecting, storing, and processing data and for delivering information, knowledge, and digital products".

Records.
It contains the main registers that store the basic typologies of the system, in accordance with what is stablished in the ExpoFinder operating document:
  • Entities [entity ENT]. The entity (according to DRAE) is understood as "any corporation, company, institution, etc., taken as a legal person". In this case, any organization with a legal entity that is used in the context of ExpoFinder, either as a source of RSS/Atom or HTML data, will be considered as such, from the results of which the system explores the Web in search of information on temporary exhibitions, as well as any other organization that acts as a support organizer or financer of an exhibition, of its catalog or to which a person is linked in a work, research and academic way.
  • Actors [person ACT]. An actor in the context of ExpoFinder is understood to be one who interacts with catalogues or exhibitions as author, editor, illustrator, curator, loan or other circumstances, and who may or may not be related to entities.
  • Catalogues [book CAT]. A catalog in the context of ExpoFinder is understood as any article, book, web page or text in general that is used to illustrate the visitor about a specific exhibition. Its “natural” use is to store information about its “catalogues”.
  • Companies [company COM]. A company in the context of ExpoFinder is understood as any organization whose relationship with entities, exhibitions, publications or individuals can be defined EXCLUSIVELY in commercial or business terms. This distinction, therefore, is not based on the public, private or mixed character of the same, nor on the fact that its purposes include or do not include a profit motive, but on the fact that the RELATIONSHIP is profitable motive.
  • Exhibitions [exhibition EXP]. An exhibition in the context of ExpoFinder is understood as any event in which a set of works of an artistic nature is exhibited before the public or whose intention is to disseminate knowledge of the academic discipline of Art History.
  • Artworks [artwork OBR]. An artwork en el contexto de ExpoFinder is understood as any manifestation of human activity through which the real is interpreted or the imagined is captured with plastic, linguistic or sound resources.
Metainformations.
It contains any additional data that allows to characterize in an unequivocal way any record. For example, its physical location, a date describing its beginning or end, or an e-mail address. In reality, and as a whole, they act as singular descriptors of any element of the record table. At the same time, metainformations make it possible to establish the links between the different types of registers, so that it is possible, for example, to locate the people who curate an exhibition or participate in it as artists.
Taxonomies.
It contains the logical categories to which a certain record is assigned. Such categories are shared with others, so that they can be used to classify in significant sets the exhibitions, companies, persons, entities and publications.

Taxonomies

In the vocabulary of Wordpress, or WP, (used in the development of ExpoFinder as an application framework), taxonomy is understood as a mechanism of classification and grouping of the different primary information-bearing records (posts in the context) based on terms and groups of terms that can respond, at the will of the developer, to a hierarchical structure. It is possible, using this application, to download the information, including terms, of all taxonomies used in ExpoFinder. Its structural organization follows the following scheme. Note that the symbol ® determines that the terms of taxonomy form a controlled vocabulary, and that the symbol § means that the taxonomy has a hierarchical structure:

  • Entities:
    • Tipology ®. Basic characterization (in quality of what acts) of the entity in the information system.
    • Ownership ®. Elementary grouping of the entity according to whether the owner or title holder of the same is under public law, private law or a mixture of both.
    • Keywords. Identifying and/or descriptive terms that can be used by the user who drafts the registration at will.
  • Actors:
    • Activity ®. Known tasks that an actor carries out that can play a significant role in the context of the application data.
  • Catalogues:
    • Editorial ®. Company or organization that acts as publisher of the publication.
    • Catalogue typology ®. Generic classification of the catalogue according to its contents and the relation with the exhibition it catalogues.
  • Companies:
    • ISIC4 Category ®. Classification of the company by its main activity. It follows the United Nations Statistics Division (UNSTAT) convention. International Standard Industrial Classification of All Economic Activities, Rev.4.
  • Exhibitions:
    • Exhibition type ®. Basic characterization of the exhibition according to hierarchy based on the works of Michael Belcher and Ángela García Blanco.
    • Artworks types ®§. Types of works exhibited according to the categories collected in the thesaurus of the Getty Foundation.
    • Topic ®§. Themes of the works exhibited according to the categories collected in the thesauri of the Getty Foundation.
    • Movements ®. Movements or artistic currents to which it is possible to ascribe the works exhibited according to the categories collected in the thesauri of the Getty Foundation.
    • Period ®. Temporary category of exhibited works based on major chronological periods (centuries and historical epochs).
  • Artworks:
    • Artworks type ®§. Typology of works exhibited according to the categories included in the thesauri of the Getty Foundation.
    • Theme ®§. Themes of the works exhibited according to the categories collected in the thesauri of the Getty Foundation.
    • Movement ®. Movements or artistic currents to which it is possible to ascribe the works exhibited in accordance with the categories included in the thesaurus of the Getty Foundation.
    • Period ®. Temporary category of exhibited works based on major chronological periods (centuries and historical epochs).

NOTE: Taxonomies of works are shared with exhibitions. Any term that is registered in one of them will be visible and usable in both types of registry.

Metadata

Each type of record recorded as custom post (entities, actors, catalogues, companies, exhibitions and works) has a considerable amount of associated meta-information, which defines its specific characteristics. As it is not purely a frouping or classifying mechanism, this meta.information is not considered taxonomy. The formal inventory of additional fields ascribed to each record type is summarized below.

In the lists below, certain conventional signs are used:

  1. The symbol ↺ means that the field is self-referenced, i.e. it searches among the records (custom post) whose abbreviation is expressed after the symbol. In this case, the fields follows the ID + : rule, meaning that each value for that field is preceded by a number that matches the ID value of the record type mentioned, followed by a colon (:) and a blank space.
  2. The symbol ⋇ means that the value of the field is verified by Geonames. If the fields is intended to store the name of a locality follows the rule ; * 2, i.e. each value must contain 3 elements separated by semicolon (;), corresponding to the following structure: municipality ; region or state ; country. If it is intended to store the name of a country, it will simply retain that value, without any separator.
  3. The symbol ⨹ means that the value of the field is verified by Google Maps.
  4. The symbol ⊠ means that the value of the field could not be modified by the user and is calculated automatically.
  5. The symbol ⊗ means that the value of the field has been automatically obtained or calculated by the Beagle process.

Those fields that must contain a date make use of the following conventions:

  1. UNIQUE DATES (a single field)
    1. If the complete date is known the format is [four digit year]-[two digit month]-[two digit day].
    2. If only month and year are known the format is [four digit year]-[two digit month]-01.
    3. If only one year is known the format is [four digit year]-01-01.
    4. If the expressed date is before the beginning of our era (B.C.) the negative sign [-] will be placed before the year.
    5. If the date alludes to a century, twenty-five-year fractions will be used and the numeral containing the beginning of the fraction will be valid, according to the following guideline:
      • NN00 = first quarter of the NN+1 century
      • NN25 = second quarter of the NN+1 century
      • NN50 = third quarter of the NN+1 century
      • NN75 = fourth (last) quarter of the NN+1 century
      Thus, for example, 1725 will be used to express the second quarter of the 18th century. Accuracies of less than 0.25 of a century will not be admitted.
  2. PAREADED DATES (beginning and end)
    1. If the two complete date are known, in both cases the format is [four digit year]-[two digit month]-[two digit day].
    2. If only the month and year of one of the two dates are known the following scheme is followed:
      1. If the start date, is known, the end date will be [four digit year]-[two digit month]-[last possible day of month].
      2. If the end date, is known, the start date will be [four digit year]-[two digit month]-01.
    3. If only one year of one of the two dates is known the following scheme is followed:
      1. If the start date is known, the end date will be [four digit year]-12-31.
      2. If the end date is known, the start date will be [four digit year]-01-01.

NOTES:

  1. The CRC32B Identifier field calculates a hash using the CRC32B algorithm on the essential fields of the record. It is used to prevent the system from trying to record the same localized exposure twice.
  2. The keywords from the list of those defined by ExpoFinder to be located by Beagle are used as a unit of measure of the reliability of finding, through a combination of calculations, which include the absolute and relative weight of localized words, according to a graduation of defined value for each of them.
  3. The CBI Error field expresses, as a percentage, the probability that a finding is erroneous, based on a calculation of a group of elementary lexemes located in the information found and according to the naïve Bayesian classifier that uses the selections and discards of data recording users as a source of valuation.
  4. The Possible Significant Marks (PMS) field identifies and extracts a part of the context in which the keywords mentioned in footnote 2 are located in order to assists data recording users in their selection and filtering task.
  5. Since not all recorded exposures come from the automatic locating process (Beagle), in the absence of the Source Entity field, prepared by Beagle, the Information source field contains the same information.
  6. The Range of visits field has the following alternatives: < 100, 100 to 499, 500 to 1.000, > 1.000.
  7. The Type of access has the following alternatives: Not available, Free for all, Free according to age criteria, Free according to numerical criteria, Free according to nationality or citizenship criteria, Paid in all cases, Mixed system, Reduced rate.
  8. The Type of relationship has the following alternatives: Main venue, Secondary venue, Auxiliary venue.
