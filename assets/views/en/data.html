To ensure a proper operation of PATHFINDER as a new knowledge getter tool, first of all it is necessary quantify database records. It means to transformate qualitative, continuous [?] and immeasurable info in quantitative, discrete and measurable values.

Groups

Aggregation functions perform statistical analyzes on numbers. An aggregation function calculates a single result value from a set of values or records, paying attention to the characteristics or meta-information that are repeated, evaluating their frequency and distribution. Use this option to know the incidence of certain parameters in the complete sample of selected values. Utilice esta opción para conocer la incidencia de ciertos parámetros en la muestra completa de valores seleccionados. IMPORTANT: all calculations are performed using only filtered values.

Relations

Using a grouping or aggregation mechanism, PATHFINDER calculates the relevance of the relationships between the various types of records. Use this option to determine the "intensity" or importance of them.

Correlations

You need to be careful with this section and understand what kind of information you can extract. It is a very powerful option to discover hidden relationships between subsets of primary registers, but it is necessary to know how to extract the significance of its results.

PATHFINDER calculates here the coincidences between two groups of primary registers related to each other from a third group. In other words, the "neighborhood" of two elements. For example, if a particular set of actors, characterized by sharing a metadata type, participates as authors of works of art in an exhibition, it would be "neighbor" of another group of actors that coincided in it participating as curators.

However, it can discover relationships, for example, between entities sharing a parent entity, whose importance may not be relevant (or may be). As an example, PATHFINDER can find links between entities managed by a high-ranking organization, such as in the case of a ministry or general management, being a sort of "useless knowledge"; however, may be the aforementioned item have a lower rank in the organization tree, info being probabily meaningful.
