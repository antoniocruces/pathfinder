In order to have PATHFINDER running as a tool to extract new knowledge is necessary, first time, to quantify database records. It means, qualitative, continuous and incommensurable information must to be transformed in quantitative, discreet and mensurable values.

Grouping

Aggregation functions perform statistical analyzes on numbers. An aggregation function calculates a single result value from a set of values or records, paying attention to repeated characteristics or metainformation, evaluaring their frequency and distribution. Use this option to know the incidence of certain parameters in the complete sample of selected values. IMPORTANT: all calculations are performed only on filtrados values.

Correlations

You need to be careful with this section and understand what kind of information you can extract. It is a very powerful option to discover hidden relationships between subsets of primary records, but it is necessary to know how to extract the significance of its results.

PATHFINDER calculates here the matches between two groups of primary records related to each other from a third group. In other words, the "neighborhood" of two elements. For example, if a certain set of actors, characterized by sharing a metadata type, participates as authors of artwork in an exhibition, it would be "neighbor" of another group of actors participating as curators.

However, it can determine that there are relationships, for example, between entities sharing a parent entity, whose importance may not be relevant (or may be). As an example, PATHFINDER can find links between entities managed by a high-ranking organization, such as in the case of a ministry or general management, and in this case be a "useless knowledge"; however, if the aforementioned organism is of a lower rank, it is possible that the discovery of such links is very useful.
