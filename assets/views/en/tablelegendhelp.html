Legend of the table. Descriptive statistics

In PATHFINDER, the Statistics section within operations with data shows a selection of statistical values that can help to better understand the distribution conditions of the analyzed sample. The abbreviations of the tables used correspond to the elements below.

For more information on the parameters contemplated in the statistical analysis, see the documentation.

FLD Field to which the data refers.
TYP Field content type: numeric, text string, date,…
FRQ Indicators concerning the frequency of the data.
  • VAL: total values (number of total rows retrieved).
  • DST: different values (number of rows with non-repeated values).
  • MIS: missing values (number of rows that do not contain a value).
RNG Indicators regarding the range of values covered by the data.
  • MIN: minimum value.
  • DST: maximum value.
  • (N): total range between the minimum and maximum value.
AVG Indicators concerning the representative values of the central tendency in the dataset.
  • MEA: arithmetic mean.
  • MED: median.
  • STD: standard deviation.
QRT Indicators referring to the extremes determining the presence of significant values.
  • Q1: first quartile limit.
  • Q3: third quartile limit.
  • MSK: mode asymmetry.
Entropy Reference of interest when judging the importance and significance of the data contained in a field. Check the documentation.
D y J Mutual distance and Jaccard index. They measure the degree of affinity or similarity between the frequencies for a given field. See the documentation about it.
